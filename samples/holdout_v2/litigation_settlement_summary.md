## Background

In April 2024, Heartwood Capital LLC filed suit against Acme Corporation
in the Delaware Court of Chancery, alleging breach of a 2021
contractual arrangement related to the licensed use of certain
algorithmic methodology and asserting damages of approximately $42M.

Acme denied the substantive allegations and counterclaimed for
declaratory judgment that no breach occurred. The matter proceeded
through limited discovery and motion practice through Q3 2025.

## Why We Are Settling

Counsel's assessment, summarized:

- **Liability:** We have a defensible position on the merits, but the
  contractual language is sufficiently ambiguous that a fact-finder
  could reasonably go either way on the central question.
- **Damages:** Even on plaintiff's most favorable construction,
  damages exceed $15M only under aggressive theories we believe a
  court would reject. But the range of plausible outcomes is wide.
- **Cost to litigate to verdict:** $4.5M–$6M in additional legal fees,
  plus management attention through Q3 2026.
- **Disclosure risk:** A trial would put internal product development
  decisions and certain customer relationships on the public record.

Settling now caps our financial exposure and removes the risk of
adverse precedent.

## Settlement Terms

- **Payment to Heartwood:** $7,250,000, paid in two tranches:
  $5,000,000 within 10 business days of signing; $2,250,000 within
  120 days
- **No admission of liability** (standard mutual non-admission clause)
- **Mutual release** covering all claims arising from the 2021
  agreement
- **Confidentiality** of settlement terms; mutual non-disparagement
  with carve-outs for required disclosures
- **Termination of underlying 2021 license** (mutually agreed); both
  parties' obligations under it conclude on the signing date
- **Cooperation on regulatory and SEC disclosures**

## Financial Impact

- **P&L hit:** $7.25M recognized in Q4 2025; flagged in the Q3
  earnings release as a contingent matter
- **Insurance recovery:** $1.5M expected; net P&L impact $5.75M
- **Cash flow:** $5M in Q4 2025; $2.25M in Q1 2026
- Below disclosure thresholds for separate 8-K filing; will be
  referenced in Q4 10-Q

## Remaining Risks

1. Plaintiff could refuse final form of release; we believe this risk
   is < 10%.
2. Insurance coverage could be challenged; coverage counsel advised
   recovery is highly likely.
3. Inadvertent disclosure by either party. Mitigation: tight
   distribution list both inside and outside Acme.
