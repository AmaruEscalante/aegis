# NON-BINDING TERM SHEET — DRAFT

**Acquirer:** Acme Corporation, a Delaware corporation ("Buyer")
**Target:** Pinemark Systems, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Company")
**Date of Draft:** October 2, 2025
**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL — Working draft. NDA-bound parties only.

---

This Term Sheet outlines the principal terms under which Buyer may
acquire the Company. This Term Sheet is non-binding except as expressly
indicated and is subject to satisfactory completion of due diligence and
the negotiation and execution of a definitive agreement.

## 1. Transaction Structure

Reverse triangular merger. Newly formed Buyer subsidiary will merge with
and into Company, with Company surviving as a wholly owned Buyer
subsidiary.

## 2. Purchase Price

**Aggregate consideration:** $186,000,000, payable as follows:

- $148,800,000 in cash at closing (80%)
- $37,200,000 in Buyer common stock (20%), valued at the trailing
  20-day VWAP ending three trading days before closing
- Up to $24,000,000 in milestone-based earn-out over 24 months,
  contingent on achieving the revenue milestones in Schedule A

## 3. Treatment of Existing Equity

- Preferred shareholders: paid liquidation preference plus participation
  per existing certificate of incorporation
- Common shareholders: pro rata of remaining consideration
- Unvested options: 50% accelerated at closing, 50% subject to
  continued service over 18 months

## 4. Retention Pool

$14,000,000 of total consideration reserved for retention awards.
Allocation:
- CEO of Company: $3,200,000, four-year vest, single-trigger
- CTO of Company: $2,400,000, four-year vest, single-trigger
- Approximately 35 named key engineers / GTM hires: balance
- Cliff: 12 months; quarterly thereafter

## 5. Working Capital Adjustment

Target net working capital: $8,000,000 (rolling 90-day average). True-up
post-closing within 60 days; collar of +/- $500,000.

## 6. Representations and Warranties

Standard representations from Company including IP, employment, tax,
data privacy, customer concentration, and material contracts. Buyer
will procure a representation and warranty insurance policy with $20M
policy limit and 0.5% retention; cost split 50/50 with Company.

## 7. Indemnification

- Cap: 10% of purchase price (excluding fundamental reps and fraud)
- Basket: $750,000 (deductible)
- Survival: 18 months (general); 6 years (fundamental); statute of
  limitations (tax)

## 8. Closing Conditions

- HSR clearance (assumed required)
- Required customer consents on top-10 contracts
- Key employee retention agreements signed
- Completion of confirmatory due diligence
- Absence of material adverse change

## 9. Exclusivity (BINDING)

Company agrees to deal exclusively with Buyer for 60 days from
execution. Company will not solicit or entertain competing offers
during the exclusivity period. Customary fiduciary out for the board.

## 10. Expenses (BINDING)

Each party bears its own expenses. If the transaction fails to close
due to Buyer's failure to obtain financing (regulatory approvals
excepted), Buyer will pay a break fee of $4,000,000.

## 11. Confidentiality (BINDING)

This Term Sheet and the negotiations are subject to the mutual NDA
dated August 8, 2025.

## 12. Process and Timeline

- Confirmatory DD: 30 days from signing
- Definitive agreement target: November 30, 2025
- Signing-to-close target: 60 days

## 13. Conditions to Signing

This Term Sheet is conditioned on:
- Board approval by both parties (Buyer board met Sept 18; pending
  Company)
- Reaffirmation of revenue forecast as of October 31, 2025
