# Q4 2025 Customer Churn Analysis

**Author:** Anjali Mehta, Senior Analyst, GTM Insights
**Reviewed by:** Cory Westbrook (VP CS), Olu Ade (Director, Revenue Ops)
**Distribution:** GTM leadership; CFO staff; CEO. DO NOT EXTERNALLY SHARE.
**Date:** January 9, 2026

## Executive Summary

Q4 2025 gross logo churn was **2.4%** (vs. Q3 1.9%; FY target 1.8%).
Gross revenue churn (excluding contraction and downgrades) was **3.1%**
(vs. Q3 2.5%). Net dollar retention dropped from **119%** to **114%**
quarter-over-quarter.

The dominant driver was a single segment — Mid-Market customers
acquired during the 2023 pricing experiment — which now represents
**62% of the gross logo churn in Q4** despite being only 18% of our
total Mid-Market base.

We recommend three specific interventions outlined in Section 5.

## 1. The Numbers

| Metric              | Q3 2025 | Q4 2025 | Change |
|---------------------|---------|---------|--------|
| Gross logo churn    | 1.9%    | 2.4%    | +0.5pp |

| Logos lost          | 87      | 112     | +29%   |

## 2. Cohort Breakdown

The "2023 pricing experiment cohort" is the set of Mid-Market customers
acquired between February and August 2023 under a 15% volume-discount
pilot. The pilot was discontinued in late 2023 but those customers
remained on grandfathered pricing.

**Q4 cohort behavior:**

- 69 logo losses out of a remaining 184 active accounts (37.5% quarterly
  loss for this cohort)
- ARR lost: $3.8M
- Stated reasons (top three, weighted by ARR):
  - Renewal pricing increase too steep (54%)
  - "Not getting enough value" (22%)
  - Switched to competitor (14%)

Note: 100% of these customers were facing a 25–40% list price increase
at renewal as their pilot pricing rolled off.

## 3. Other Drivers

- **SMB acquired-in-Q1 cohort** continues to underperform; 4.1% Q4
  churn. This is consistent with the lower-quality leads from the
  Q1 paid-acquisition channel and was expected.
- **Enterprise segment** churn unchanged at 0.6% gross logo. Healthy.
- **Industry vertical:** Manufacturing customers churned at 2x other
  verticals; appears tied to the Acme Manufacturing Suite product gaps
  identified in the Q3 product review.

## 4. What We Don't Know

- We do NOT have a controlled comparison for the discount-roll-off
  cohort. We can describe the population but cannot prove that the
  price increase alone caused the churn.
- We don't yet have win-back data for Q4 losses (typically lags 90
  days). Q3 win-back rate was 8%.

## 5. Recommendations

1. **Re-price the discount-roll-off cohort with a graduated 18-month
   transition.** Modeled ARR retention: 71% of remaining cohort.
   Modeled cost: $2.1M in foregone price increase. Net positive vs.
   continued churn projection.
2. **Tighten SMB acquisition criteria.** Pause the Q1 channel that
   produced the underperforming cohort; reallocate spend.
3. **Manufacturing product gap roadmap.** Three specific features have
   been requested in 8 of the 11 manufacturing churn calls. Product
   review with engineering required.

## 6. Methodology

Data extracted from the warehouse on Jan 5, 2026. Definitions follow
the FY24 metrics dictionary v3. Logo churn measured on quarterly
anniversary; revenue churn on actual billing events.
