## TL;DR

We expect Q4 to land in the **$72.0M – $76.5M new ARR range**, vs. the
$78M plan committed to the board. The midpoint of our forecast
($74.2M) represents a $3.8M miss against plan, or ~5%.

This memo decomposes the gap, assesses sensitivity, and recommends a
revised board guidance.

## 1. Forecast Build

| Component                | Plan    | Forecast (mid) | Variance |
|--------------------------|---------|----------------|----------|
| Enterprise new logo      | $24M    | $26.5M         | +$2.5M   |
| Enterprise expansion     | $18M    | $14M           | -$4M     |
| Mid-market new logo      | $22M    | $19.5M         | -$2.5M   |
| Mid-market expansion     | $9M     | $9.5M          | +$0.5M   |

| Total new ARR            | $78M    | $74.2M         | -$3.8M   |

## 2. What's Driving the Gap

**Enterprise expansion underperformance ($4M short):**
Three large logos that we forecast for expansion in Q4 have slipped:
- TX-2451: $1.6M expansion; CFO turnover at customer; reset to Q1
- NW-1827: $1.1M expansion; security review extended; expected Q1
- BR-3091: $1.3M expansion; customer is doing a competitive review;
  outcome uncertain, removed from forecast

**Mid-market new logo ($2.5M short):**
We are seeing longer sales cycles in mid-market — average days-in-stage
"Proof of Value" has grown from 24 days in Q1 to 39 days in Q3. The
discount-roll-off cohort (see separate churn memo) is consuming CS
attention that historically would have been on new sales support.

**Enterprise new logo upside (+$2.5M):**
Two enterprise deals we forecast as Q1 closed in late October. This
will be a Q1 hole if not refilled.

## 3. Sensitivity Analysis

Bear case ($72M): BR-3091 churns AND we miss two of our top 5
pipeline deals.

Base case ($74.2M): BR-3091 stays but doesn't expand; pipeline closes
at historical rate.

Bull case ($76.5M): BR-3091 expands at smaller-than-planned $0.6M;
pipeline closes at 110% of historical rate.

## 4. Cash and Margin Implications

- Cash flow: Q4 cash flow plan unchanged; collections are tracking on
  plan, and we have $2M of pull-forward potential from prepays
- Gross margin: tracking 76.4% (vs. 76.0% plan), driven by improving
  hosting unit costs
- Operating margin: largely unchanged due to disciplined hiring pace

## 5. Recommended Board Communication

Recommend revising Q4 guidance at the December board meeting:

- New ARR: $72M – $76M (midpoint $74M) — down from $78M
- ARR exit: $260M (was $264M) — still on track for the FY25 commitment
  of $258M – $268M
- Frame as: "two large expansions slipped one quarter, not a
  fundamental demand change"
