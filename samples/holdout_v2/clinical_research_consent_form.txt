PURPOSE OF THIS RESEARCH

You are being invited to take part in a clinical research study because
you have been diagnosed with chronic migraine that has not improved with
at least three previous preventive treatments. The purpose of this
research is to evaluate whether the investigational drug CMB-417 reduces
the number of migraine days per month and whether it is safe to use.

About 120 adults aged 18 to 65 will participate at this and four other
sites in the United States.

PROCEDURES

If you decide to participate, you will be asked to:

  - Attend approximately 9 study visits over 24 weeks.
  - Take CMB-417 by mouth once daily for 16 weeks.
  - Keep an electronic headache diary.
  - Provide blood samples at five visits for safety labs.
  - Allow the study team to access your prior medical records relevant
    to your migraine history.

RISKS

Common side effects reported in earlier trials include mild nausea,
fatigue, and dry mouth. Less common but more serious risks include
liver enzyme elevation and one reported case of allergic reaction.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Your records will be kept confidential to the extent allowed by law.
Study staff, the sponsor, and regulatory bodies (FDA, IRB) may inspect
your records. Any publication of study results will not identify you
personally.

VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time
without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.
