This document captures the narrative we want to land in our Series B
roadshow. Each slide will have a final designed version; this is the
internal logic and talking points behind each slide. Do not share with
anyone outside Acme without David's explicit approval.

## Slide 1 — Cover

Logo, tagline ("Infrastructure for the operations layer of every
business"), CEO name and title. Talking point: open with the customer
testimonial from the Marston call.

## Slide 2 — The Problem

- Operations teams at growth-stage companies run on a Frankenstein of
  spreadsheets, Zapier, and in-house scripts.
- This costs them: typical $2M+/year in shadow ops headcount, 8+ hours/
  week of senior PM time, and constant data drift.
- The market we sell to has tripled since 2022 and shows no signs of
  consolidation.

**Talking point:** Lead with a specific customer story (Northwind),
not statistics. Numbers come on slide 3.

## Slide 3 — The Market

- TAM: $48B by 2028 (Gartner; we adjusted with our own bottom-up build)
- SAM (English-language, > $20M ARR companies): $14B
- SOM (current product capability): $1.2B
- Growth: 28% CAGR

**Talking point:** Be prepared for "your TAM is too generous." Have the
bottom-up math on the next slide if pressed.

## Slide 4 — Our Solution (current product)

- Three-pillar overview: Workflow, Data, Insights
- Live demo: 60 seconds of product, no slides
- Customer logos: 12 logos, sequenced by recognition

**Talking point:** Live demo MUST be < 60 seconds. We have lost more
attention with demos than we've ever gained.

## Slide 5 — Why Now

- AI agents create new operational complexity (orchestration, hand-off,
  audit) that we are uniquely positioned to manage.
- Old workflow vendors are stuck on per-seat pricing in a per-action
  world.
- Mid-market companies are explicitly demanding what we sell, in our
  win calls.

## Slide 6 — Traction

- ARR: $48M growing 220% YoY
- Net dollar retention: 134% (DO NOT round)
- Gross margin: 76%
- Magic number: 1.4
- Logos: 1,420; enterprise: 84
- Burn multiple: 0.6

**Talking point:** This is the slide investors look at first. We will
spend 4 minutes here.

## Slide 7 — Go-to-Market

- Three-motion model: Self-serve, Inside Sales, Enterprise
- Self-serve → Inside Sales graduation: 38% in 6 months
- Inside Sales → Enterprise graduation: 19% in 12 months
- CAC payback: 13 months blended, 9 months on Enterprise

## Slide 8 — The Team

- Founding team bios (David, Trevor, Margaret)
- Heads of GTM (Cory), Eng (Pratima), Product (Sasha)
- Total team: 142; engineers: 64
- Notable hires this year: Sasha (ex-Linear PM lead), three principal
  engineers from prior unicorns

## Slide 9 — The Ask

- $80M Series B
- Use of funds:
  - 50% — engineering scale (platform, AI agent infra)
  - 30% — GTM expansion (Enterprise team, EMEA opening)
  - 15% — finance, legal, BizOps infrastructure
  - 5% — opportunistic M&A reserve
- Target valuation: $720M post (~9x ARR forward multiple)

**Talking point:** We are NOT leading with valuation in conversations.
Anchor first on the business; valuation discussion is for the second
meeting.

## Slide 10 — Vision (long-term)

- Three-horizon framing (1-year, 3-year, 7-year)
- What "winning" looks like
- The acquisition adjacency
