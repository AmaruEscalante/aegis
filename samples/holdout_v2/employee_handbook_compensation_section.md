# Compensation Philosophy & Practices
## Acme Corporation Employee Handbook — Section 7
### Internal Use Only — Not For External Distribution

---

This section describes how Acme structures compensation. It is intended
to give every employee a clear picture of how pay decisions are made and
what to expect across the employee lifecycle. This is an internal
document; please do not share externally.

## 7.1 Our Compensation Philosophy

Acme pays competitively for the role, the market, and the level. We
target the **65th percentile of total cash** for our defined peer group,
with the explicit understanding that:

- Equity is a meaningful portion of total comp at every level.
- We do not negotiate against ourselves with counter-offers from
  competitors as a regular practice. We make our best offer up front.
- We adjust compensation transparently at annual cycles. We do not run
  individual mid-cycle adjustments except in narrow cases (promotion,
  scope expansion of one full level, or correction of a market error).

## 7.2 Total Compensation Structure

Total compensation has four components:

1. **Base salary** — paid biweekly, reviewed annually.
2. **Annual bonus target** — percentage of base, paid in Q1 of the
   following year based on company performance multiplier × individual
   performance multiplier.
3. **Equity** — restricted stock units (RSUs) with a 4-year vest,
   1-year cliff, quarterly thereafter, granted at hire and refreshed
   annually starting in year 2.
4. **Benefits** — covered separately in Section 8.

## 7.3 Levels and Ranges

Acme uses a level framework from L2 (entry IC) to L9 (executive). Each
level has a defined band for base, bonus target, and new-hire equity.
The bands are reviewed annually against three benchmarking sources and
adjusted by the Compensation Committee.

Bands are NOT shared individually with employees, but every employee
can ask their manager what level they are at and what level they are
working toward. Managers are required to answer.

## 7.4 Bonus Mechanics

- Bonus target ranges from 10% of base (L2) to 35% of base (L8).
- Company multiplier set by the board in Q1 based on the prior year's
  performance against company-wide objectives.
- Individual multiplier set by manager in the year-end review cycle.
- Payout = base × target × company × individual.
- Bonuses are at-will; payment requires employment on the payment date.

## 7.5 Equity Practices

- New-hire grants sized by level and offer competitiveness.
- Annual refresh grants typically sized at 25–40% of original new-hire
  grant value, weighted by performance and tenure.
- Promotion grants when moving up a level: typically equivalent to a
  one-time small new-hire grant at the new level.
- Top performers may receive out-of-cycle "exceptional impact" grants
  at the manager's nomination + skip-level approval. These are uncommon
  by design.

## 7.6 Promotion Calibration

Promotions happen twice per year, in May and November. Managers
nominate; the decision is calibrated cross-functionally. The board
approves all VP+ promotions.

## 7.7 Pay Transparency

We post salary ranges on internal job postings. We are working toward
posting ranges on external postings; this is currently active in
California, Colorado, Washington, New York City, and any other
jurisdiction where required by law.

## 7.8 Questions

Direct compensation questions to your manager or to comp@acme.internal.
Disputes about a specific decision can be escalated to People Ops or to
the ombudsperson described in Section 12.
