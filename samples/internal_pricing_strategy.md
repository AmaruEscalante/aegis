# Pricing Strategy Q3 2026 — Internal Working Draft

**Status:** internal-confidential — do not share outside Pricing Working Group + Exec Staff
**Owner:** S. Patel (VP Revenue)
**Last revised:** 2026-05-08

## Context

Following the May exec offsite, we agreed to revisit our pricing model before the Q3 board meeting on August 11. This document captures the working group's current direction, open questions, and the data behind both. It is not a final decision.

## Current state (April 2026)

- 3 tiers: Starter ($29/mo), Growth ($149/mo), Scale ($899/mo).
- 73% of revenue from Growth tier; Scale conversion at 4.2% (down from 6.1% YoY).
- Net revenue retention: 108% (target: 115%).
- Gross margin: 71% (acceptable but eroded 4pts since the infra rebuild).

## Working group recommendation

**Move to usage-based metering layered over the existing tier structure.**

The proposal is to keep $29 / $149 / $899 as inclusion floors, but add per-unit metering above included quotas:
- Starter: 5K events/mo included, $0.004/event over.
- Growth: 100K events/mo included, $0.0025/event over.
- Scale: 2M events/mo included, $0.0012/event over.

Modeling (see appendix A) projects this lifts net revenue per customer by 14-22% across the cohort, with the largest gains concentrated in the bottom half of the Growth tier where customers are currently 2-3x over their tier's "natural" usage band.

## Open questions

1. **Migration mechanics for existing customers.** Three options under debate (Appendix B). Legal is reviewing whether tier-level grandfathering creates a contractual obligation that locks us out of future repricing.
2. **Competitor signaling.** Crowdsoft moved to a similar model in November and reported 4-6% churn spike at announcement. We should model that downside.
3. **Disclosure timing.** Marketing wants 90-day customer notice; Finance prefers 30-day to capture Q3 numbers. CEO leaning toward 60-day.
