## Overview

This briefing covers our four principal competitors in the mid-market workflow automation segment.
Win/loss data sourced from Salesforce CRM (Q4 2025 close analysis) and 12 customer interviews.

## Competitor A: Flowbridge (est. 2019, ~$85M ARR)

**Strengths:**
- Deepest Salesforce integration in the category; won 4 deals we lost in Q4 purely on SFDC connector
- Strong brand in RevOps community; David Kwan's LinkedIn presence drives significant inbound
- Lowest TCO pricing for SMB segment (<50 seats)

**Weaknesses:**
- Engineering quality declining; 3 customers migrating to us in H1 2026 citing reliability
- No native AI features as of Jan 2026; roadmap leaked to us via job postings shows 6-month delay
- High churn in enterprise (>1,000 seat) segment — 34% annual churn per their former CSM (left in Dec)

**Our Win Rate vs. Flowbridge:** 38% (up from 29% in Q3 2025)
**Key Battlecard:** Emphasize reliability SLA and AI roadmap; do NOT compete on price.

## Competitor B: Orion Works (est. 2021, ~$31M ARR, well-funded Series C at $140M valuation)

**Strengths:**
- Exceptional developer experience; high NPS among technical buyers
- Cheap at PLG entry level; aggressive expansion revenue model

**Weaknesses:**
- Immature enterprise security posture (SOC 2 Type II only received Nov 2025)
- Single-threaded leadership risk: CEO/CTO departure rumors (two sources)
- No phone/voice support; enterprise procurement teams are blocked

**Our Win Rate vs. Orion:** 61%
**Key Battlecard:** Lead with SOC 2 + ISO 27001 dual certification and dedicated CSM.
